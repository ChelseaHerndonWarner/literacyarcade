Literacy Arcade

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: July 5, 2026

Plain-language summary: Literacy Arcade is designed to minimize student data collection. Students do not create accounts, log in, or provide email addresses to use student-facing games and activities. Teacher accounts, if offered, are optional and intended for adult educators, tutors, interventionists, and parents.

1. Overview

Literacy Arcade provides literacy games and teacher tools for classroom, tutoring, intervention, and at-home reading practice. We are committed to limiting unnecessary data collection, especially from students.

2. Student Use

Students do not have accounts on Literacy Arcade. Students are not required to provide names, email addresses, usernames, passwords, or other personal information to use student-facing games and activities.

Some tools may allow a teacher or user to type words, passages, reading scores, student names, or other information directly into a tool. Unless a teacher account/save feature is specifically used, this information is used only within the tool during that session and is not intentionally stored by Literacy Arcade on our servers.

Teachers and adults should avoid entering full student names or sensitive student information into any tool. If a student identifier is needed, teachers should use initials, first names, student numbers, or another classroom identifier.

3. Teacher Accounts and Saved Content

Literacy Arcade may offer optional teacher accounts so adult users can save word lists, settings, lesson materials, or other teacher-created content. Students will not have accounts.

If a teacher chooses to sign in with Google, Literacy Arcade may receive limited Google account information such as the teacher’s name, email address, and profile image, depending on the permissions granted. This information is used to provide account access and save teacher-created materials.

Saved content is intended for teacher-created instructional materials, such as word lists or tool settings. Teachers should not save sensitive student information, full student names, student records, disability information, grades, or other protected student data in Literacy Arcade.

Teachers may share an activity with students using a link or the official Google Classroom sharing button. When an activity is shared, its title, selected words or sentences, settings, and game setup are accessible to anyone who has the link. Shared activity links use a random, opaque identifier and are not intended to be browsable, searchable, or listed publicly — Literacy Arcade does not provide any way to look up or list shared activities without the specific link. Because a shared activity's title and content are accessible to anyone with the link, teachers should not use student names or other identifying student information in activity titles, word lists, sentence sets, or other saved or shared materials.

4. Information We May Collect

Depending on how the site is used, Literacy Arcade may collect or process:

  • Teacher account information, such as name, email address, and profile image, if a teacher chooses to sign in with Google.
  • Teacher-created saved content, such as saved word lists, lesson lists, tool settings, or feedback.
  • Messages or contact information submitted voluntarily through forms, email, or beta sign-up pages.
  • Usage and device information through analytics tools (such as Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity), such as pages visited, approximate location, browser type, device type, referring site, and general site activity. These analytics tools are not loaded on student-facing shared activity pages — see Section 5.

5. Google Analytics, Microsoft Clarity, and Cookies

Literacy Arcade uses Google Analytics on teacher-facing pages to understand how visitors use the site, such as which pages are visited and which tools are used most often. This helps us improve the website and decide which literacy tools to build next.

Literacy Arcade also uses Microsoft Clarity on teacher-facing pages. Clarity helps us understand site usage through aggregated behavior analytics, such as session recordings and heatmaps, so we can improve navigation and tool design.

Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity are not loaded on student-facing shared activity pages — the pages a student reaches through a teacher's locked, shared activity link (identified by an "activity" parameter in the web address). Analytics on shareable tools is limited to the teacher-facing setup and dashboard experience.

Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity may use cookies or similar technologies to measure site activity. We do not use Google Analytics or Microsoft Clarity to collect student names, student email addresses, classroom rosters, or student records.

6. How Information Is Used

Information may be used to operate and improve Literacy Arcade, provide optional teacher account access and saved teacher-created content, respond to feedback or beta requests, understand general site usage, and maintain site security.

7. Information Sharing

Literacy Arcade does not sell personal information. We may use trusted third-party services, such as Google Sign-In, Google Analytics, Microsoft Clarity, website hosting providers, or form tools, to operate and improve the website. These providers may process limited information according to their own privacy practices.

Literacy Arcade uses Firebase and Google Cloud (services provided by Google) to power Google sign-in, store teacher-saved activities and dashboard materials, and host shared activity links. Firebase and Google Cloud process this information according to Google's own privacy and security practices.

Some word and vocabulary tools, including Word Forge, may check a typed word against a third-party dictionary lookup service (api.dictionaryapi.dev) to confirm it is a valid word. This may send the typed word itself to that service; no student name or other identifying information is included in that request.

8. Children’s Privacy

Literacy Arcade is designed to avoid collecting personal information from children. Students do not create accounts and are not asked to provide email addresses or other account information. If we learn that personal information from a child has been collected unintentionally, we will take reasonable steps to delete it.

9. Data Security

Literacy Arcade uses reasonable efforts and trusted third-party services to protect information. However, no website or internet transmission can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

10. Data Access and Deletion

Teachers who create an account may request access to, correction of, or deletion of their account information and saved teacher-created content by contacting Literacy Arcade.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as Literacy Arcade grows or as features change. When we make changes, we will update the effective date at the top of this page.

12. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or want to request deletion of account information, please contact Literacy Arcade through the contact method provided on the website.
